§10.3 · The crisis opens the door
04 / 13
Two hurdles for new entrants — regulation and trust

2008–09 lowered both barriers.

Regulation eased. Banking is a low-margin commodity business (profit margins below 2% on average) that only generates returns through leverage — and high leverage means high risk. Post-crisis, angry voters pushed governments to ring-fence banks and to boost competition: many adopted open banking and waved through fintech start-ups.
Trust collapsed. A 2012 Guardian survey put trust in banking at a five-year low — 71% felt banks had not learned their lesson. The damage was worst among the young: a 2016 survey found 71% of millennials would rather visit the dentist than listen to their banks.

The crisis also unleashed a wave of innovation. Disgruntled insiders left to launch fintech start-ups — they knew exactly where the industry profit pools were located and set about draining them while incumbents were weak and customers were willing to switch.
