“Data is the new oil” — but banks hoard it

From screen scraping to APIs.

The data belongs to the customer, yet banks guard it. The traditional workaround — screen scraping — forces consumers to hand over their login and password, violating user agreements and creating real security risk. More than 4 million Canadians have done it anyway.

Screen scraping creates “significant risk to consumers’ financial health, including the potential for account takeover, unauthorized payment transactions, and identity theft.”
— US bank CEO, written Senate testimony
The safer alternative · open banking via API
An API lets two computers exchange data securely over a network — used inside banks and with partners like VISA and MasterCard for decades.
Open banking came into force in 2018 in the UK and EU, with countries worldwide following. It gives customers control: a bank must share data with an authorized third party via a secure API, and the customer can revoke access at any time.
A double-edged sword. Research finds open banking promotes competition when it levels borrower screening — but if it hands fintechs monopoly power over data, it can hinder competition and leave all borrowers worse off.
