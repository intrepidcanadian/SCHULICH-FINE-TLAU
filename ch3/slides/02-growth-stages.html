Capital lifecycle

Three stages of growth.

Every fintech traverses a predictable capital arc: early-stage survival funding, growth-stage scaling capital, and late-stage liquidity events. Each stage carries distinct risk profiles, investor types, and governance trade-offs.

Early Stage
Pre-Seed → Seed → Series A
Bootstrapping, FFF, angels. Proving product-market fit through the Valley of Death.
Growth Stage
Series B → Series F
VC firms deploy $10M–$500M+ rounds. Focus shifts to market dominance and unit-economics proof.
Late Stage
Pre-IPO → IPO / Acquisition
Liquidity events for early investors. IPO (Coinbase, Robinhood) or acquisition (Plaid by Visa, $5.3B blocked).
