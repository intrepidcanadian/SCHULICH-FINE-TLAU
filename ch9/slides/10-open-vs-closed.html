The real question: is the network also the bank?

Open four-party vs. closed three-party.

Open · four-party — VISA / Mastercard
You · cardholder
↓
Issuing bank · your bank
↕ routed via the network (Visa / MC) ↕
Acquiring bank · merchant’s bank — a different company
↓
Merchant
Issuer ≠ acquirer → the network sets an interchange fee between the two banks.
Closed · three-party — Amex / Discover
You · cardholder
↓
American Express
issuer + acquirer + network — one company
↓
Merchant
One company on both sides → no interchange; it just charges a single, higher merchant fee.
Open — Visa / Mastercard Closed — Amex / Discover
PartiesFour — you, issuer, acquirer, merchant (network in the middle)Three — you, the network-bank, merchant
InterchangeYes — acquirer pays the issuer (set by the network)None — nothing sits “between banks”
Merchant costLower (competitive / often regulated)Higher (one all-in fee)
Reach & dataNear-universal acceptance; rewards vary by bankNarrower; sees both sides → richer data & rewards
