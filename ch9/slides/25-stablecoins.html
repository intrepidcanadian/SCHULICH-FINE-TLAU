Recall Beaver → Eagle’s four routes (slide 15) — now there is a credible fifth

Stablecoins entered the mainstream of payments.

In 2022 a crypto cross-border transfer was a fringe option. By mid-2026 stablecoins are a ~$315B+ market that settled an estimated $33 trillion on-chain in 2025 (gross) — and, for the first time, they have legal footing in the US.

The networks moved in
PayPal & Ripple
PayPal’s PYUSD launched Aug 2023 (~$3B); Ripple’s RLUSD launched Dec 2024 — updating the chapter’s RippleNet reference.
Stripe · Bridge
Stripe bought stablecoin platform Bridge for $1.1B (closed Feb 2025) — its largest deal; stablecoin accounts now in 100+ countries.
Visa
Began USDC settlement for US banks (Dec 2025) over Solana; ~$3.5B annualized run-rate.
Mastercard
Merchant USDC settlement since Apr 2025, plus stablecoin payouts and acquirer settlement.

The GENIUS Act (signed Jul 2025) is the first US federal framework for payment stablecoins: 100% reserve backing in cash or short-term Treasuries, monthly public reserve disclosure, and AML compliance. Legitimacy — not just technology — is what changed.
