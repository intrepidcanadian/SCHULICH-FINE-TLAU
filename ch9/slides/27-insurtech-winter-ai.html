The reckoning after the 2021 boom — and where the money went

From funding winter to AI.

The slide on IPO performance argued the business model, not the technology, decides success. The years since proved it — funding froze ~76% from the peak, and only the insurtechs that reached profitability survived.

Survivors found profit. Root posted its first profitable full year (2024, ~$31M); Oscar Health its first ever (2024, $25.4M); Hippo its first GAAP-profitable quarter (Q4 2024). Lemonade is still loss-making but turned cash-flow positive in Q4 2025, targeting profit by 2027.
Capital rotated to AI. AI-focused insurtechs took ~47% of all 2024 insurtech funding, and by late 2025 a majority of new insurance-AI deployments were generative or agentic — automating the underwriting and claims the chapter flagged as the core pain points.
