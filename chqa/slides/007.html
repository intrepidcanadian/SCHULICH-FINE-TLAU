Chapter 5 · Bitcoin, Blockchain & Crypto

What are the transaction fees associated with Bitcoin and how are they determined and allocated among miners?

Bitcoin imposes no official transaction fees, but users offer voluntary fees to incentivize miners to prioritize their transactions from the mempool.
—Fees arose after mid-2016 when transactions exceeded the 1-MB block size, causing congestion.
—Miners choose transactions with the highest fee-to-size ratio to maximize each block's aggregate fee.
—The fee, called the feerate, is measured in Satoshis-per-byte; 1 bitcoin equals 100,000 Satoshis.
—Fees became de facto mandatory and are highly volatile as the network became more crowded.
