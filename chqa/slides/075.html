What was Facebook's Libra project and what happened to it?

Libra, announced June 2019, was a Facebook-operated stablecoin backed by fiat currencies and US Treasuries, designed for low-cost global money transfers over Messenger and WhatsApp.
—Launched with 28 partners but notably had no bank partner.
—Regulators in the US and Europe attacked it and called for hearings; the G7 formed a taskforce.
—Partners dropped out starting with PayPal, then MasterCard, VISA, eBay, and Stripe.
—Rebranded as Diem in December 2020, it was shut down in January 2022 and its code sold.
