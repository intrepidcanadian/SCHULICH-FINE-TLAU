Chapter 8 · Digital Wealth

What is passive investing and how does it explain the growth of exchange traded funds (ETFs)?

Passive (index) investing tracks broad market indices and minimizes fees rather than trying to beat markets through selection or timing, which fueled ETF growth as cheap index-tracking vehicles.
—Grounded in modern portfolio theory by Markowitz, Sharpe; Bogle created first index fund in 1975.
—Only one-quarter of active funds beat passive rivals over the decade ending 2021.
—Active funds fell from 81% of the market in 2010 to 60% in 2020.
—ETFs charge much lower MER (0.03%-1.00%), no minimum, trade all day; passed $10 trillion, 8,500+ funds.
