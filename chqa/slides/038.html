Chapter 8 · Digital Wealth

What is the business strategy of robo-advisors and what customers are they targeting?

Robo-advisors followed a textbook disruptive innovation strategy, gaining a foothold by targeting two underserved segments with low fees, a great digital experience, and technology-enabled features.
—Underserved millennials/HENRYs: tech-savvy, low savings (~$10,000), distrustful of banks.
—Price-sensitive mass-affluent: older DIY investors with $100,000-$1,000,000 unwilling to pay high fees.
—Value proposition: delightful experience, transparent low fees, state-of-the-art tech, tax-loss harvesting.
—Later moved up-market and added a human advisor option for higher fees.
