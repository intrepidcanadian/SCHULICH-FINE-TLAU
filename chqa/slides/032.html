Chapter 7 · Crowdfunding & P2P

How successful have crowdfunding platforms been in disrupting traditional financial intermediaries such as banks?

Rather than disrupting traditional capital raising, online portals became an alternative digital channel, with many pioneers forced to pivot after years of losses.
—Running a portal is tough, with customer acquisition costs of $500 to $1,000 each.
—Public portals like LendingClub and OnDeck all fell below their IPO listing prices.
—LendingClub exited P2P entirely in 2021 to relaunch as a challenger bank.
—Portals reduced information asymmetry but failed on liquidity, risk sharing, and trust.
