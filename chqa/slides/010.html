Chapter 5 · Bitcoin, Blockchain & Crypto

What are cryptowallets? What are cryptoexchanges?

A cryptowallet is a software application or hardware device that stores a private key or seed to send and receive cryptocurrencies, while a cryptoexchange is an electronic marketplace for buying and selling them for a fee.
—Wallets are hot (internet-connected software) or more secure cold storage like the Trezor hardware wallet.
—Wallets do not hold coins but store a secret seed used to generate addresses and authorize transactions.
—Exchanges include fiat-to-crypto and crypto-to-crypto types; leaders are Binance, Coinbase, and Kraken.
—Exchanges hold customers' private keys, becoming honey pots vulnerable to hacks like Mt. Gox's 850,000 bitcoins.
