Chapter 8 · Digital Wealth

What is a robo-advisor and how do they create a personalized portfolio for a customer? What does it cost to use a robo-advisor?

A robo-advisor is an automated online investment portal that uses algorithms to map a customer's answers about goals, returns, and risk tolerance to a portfolio recommendation, charging low fees.
—You answer questions online about savings goals, return expectations, and risk tolerance.
—Algorithm builds a balanced portfolio of low-cost ETFs, auto-rebalanced quarterly, monthly, or daily.
—Advisory fees typically run 0.25% to 0.50% of AUM, first emerging in 2008.
—Customer also pays the third-party ETF's MER, making an all-in cost around 0.62%-0.73%.
