Chapter 7 · Crowdfunding & P2P

Has crowdfunding increased access to capital for underbanked communities? Are there biases based on gender, race, or other factors?

Evidence is mixed on financial inclusion, and research finds racial biases in lending pricing and funding, though gender findings are more nuanced.
—Some studies show portals fill gaps in underbanked, rural areas with fewer bank branches.
—Other studies find lenders target creditworthy borrowers already with bank access, charging higher rates to lower-rated ones.
—Latinx and African American borrowers were charged higher mortgage rates after adjusting for risk.
—On Kickstarter women had higher success rates; African American men in equity crowdfunding are less likely to receive funding.
