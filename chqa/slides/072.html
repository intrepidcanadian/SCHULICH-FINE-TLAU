Chapter 11 · Techfins & Bigtech

What is a bigtech and how do they compare in size to the incumbent financial institutions such as banks?

Bigtech refers to North American global technology companies (Amazon, Apple, Facebook, Google) whose stock market capitalization is far larger than the world's largest financial institutions.
—They dwarf incumbents like VISA, JPMorgan, MasterCard, Bank of America, and ICBC.
—Their strength is vast multi-sided platforms, billions of customers, and massive datasets.
—In 2022's most valuable brands, bigtech held the top five; JPMorgan was first bank at #24.
—Apple's brand value reached US$482 billion, versus banks lagging far behind.
