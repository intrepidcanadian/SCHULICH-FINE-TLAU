Chapter 9 · Payments & Insurance

What are the different segments of the insurance market and how can we assess their relative size?

Insurance is a US$5.7 trillion (2020) industry measured by gross written premiums, split into life (42%), property and casualty (31%), and health (27%) insurance.
—Size is measured by gross written premiums, the dollar value of premiums paid.
—Life insurance pays a lump sum on disability or premature death.
—P&C covers property damage plus casualty risks like auto, theft, and liability.
—Two B2B profit pools: distribution/policy management and reinsurance.
