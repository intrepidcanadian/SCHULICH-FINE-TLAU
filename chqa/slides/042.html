Chapter 8 · Digital Wealth

What is the difference between institutional investors and retail customers? What are high net worth customers?

Institutional investors are financial intermediaries paid to manage portfolios on the wholesale side, while retail customers are individual investors; high net worth customers hold $1 million or more in investments.
—Institutional investors include asset managers, pension funds, mutual funds, insurers, and banks.
—Paid on AUM (they decide) or AUA (client decides), with fees up to 3.50%.
—Retail investors are individuals advised by financial advisors, RIAs, planners, or counselors.
—HNW is $1 million+, UHNW is $30 million+; UHNW often set up family offices.
