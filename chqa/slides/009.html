What is a hard fork and how did one lead to the creation of Bitcoin Cash?

A hard fork is a disagreement among cryptocurrency holders where a minority breaks away to create a new coin on a new blockchain, splitting the original like a stock spin-off.
—Both coins share the same transaction history up to the point of the fork.
—Bitcoin Cash forked on August 1, 2017, at block 478,558 on the Bitcoin blockchain.
—A minority wanted an 8-MB block size to process more transactions per 10 minutes.
—After the fork, one Bitcoin was ~$2,741 and one Bitcoin Cash ~$380, totaling $3,121.
