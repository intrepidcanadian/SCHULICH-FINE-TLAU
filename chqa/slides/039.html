Chapter 8 · Digital Wealth

What has been the experience of incumbent asset managers as they respond to the threat of disruption from robo-advisors?

Incumbents were fast-followers who built in-house or licensed robo software and, despite starting later, captured most of the robo-advisory market's assets.
—Vanguard responded first in 2013, Schwab in 2014, then Bank of America, Fidelity, TD Ameritrade in 2016.
—Followed Christensen's advice: respond with a new low-cost division without abandoning core customers.
—Vanguard's robo reached $268 billion by 2022, 4.6x combined Wealthfront and Betterment.
—Others licensed (SigFig to UBS), white-labeled, or acquired (BlackRock bought FutureAdvisor).
