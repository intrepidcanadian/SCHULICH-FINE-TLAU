Chapter 9 · Payments & Insurance

What are the payment rails and what is the difference between the large value transfer system and the system for small payments?

Payment rails are the physical network of cables and computers routing electronic payments; the LVTS handles large business payments fast, while small-payment systems handle high-volume consumer payments slowly.
—LVTS transfers large sums between banks in one day or intraday, enabling real-time gross settlement.
—US LVTS: Fedwire and CHIPS; Canada: Lynx; Europe: TARGET2.
—Small payments use ACH (US), ACSS (Canada), EUCHA (Europe) for checks and cards.
—Small-payment 'local roads' are slower, taking days to complete.
