How did the 2008-2009 Global Financial Crisis lower the barriers to entry for fintechs into banking?

The crisis lowered the two main hurdles of trust and regulation, destroying public confidence in banks and prompting policies that encouraged new entrants.
—A 2012 Guardian survey found 71% thought banks had not learned their lesson.
—Bailouts and recession spurred open banking legislation forcing banks to share customer data.
—Politicians eased or ignored regulatory requirements for fintech start-ups to boost competition.
—Disgruntled industry insiders left to launch fintech start-ups draining incumbent profit pools.
