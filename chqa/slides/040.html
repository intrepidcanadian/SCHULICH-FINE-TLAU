Chapter 8 · Digital Wealth

What are the main lessons from the David-vs.-Goliath battle between robo-advisors and incumbents?

The main lessons are that acquiring customers is the biggest obstacle, technology is not a sustainable competitive advantage, and it may be smarter to partner with incumbents than attack them.
—Customer acquisition costs upwards of $500 each, giving a 14.4-year payback on a $10,000 account.
—Incumbents have massive customer bases, brands, distribution, and cross-selling to subsidize robos.
—Technology is widely available and copyable; it is not the ultimate value proposition.
—Pivoting to B2B works: Wealthsimple for Advisors and Nest Wealth partnered with incumbents.
