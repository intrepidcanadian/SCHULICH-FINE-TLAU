Chapter 10 · Digital Banking

What has been the go-to-market strategy of challenger banks and are they regulated?

Challenger banks build a foothold with one product in their home market then rebundle and expand abroad; regulation splits between those holding a banking license and those without.
—N26 started with a free deposit account and debit card; Revolut began as a licensed MSB targeting travelers.
—A banking license is needed for insured deposits and central bank support, but loans and credit cards are unregulated.
—Unlicensed banks like Chime must partner with licensed banks (Bancorp Bank, Stride Bank).
—Many expand into neighboring countries and partner with fintechs via APIs to add products.
