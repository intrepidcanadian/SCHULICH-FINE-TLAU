Chapter 10 · Digital Banking

What is the business model and monetization strategy of a financial marketplace?

Financial marketplaces are multi-sided platforms that connect customers with third-party products via APIs and open banking, monetizing through fees, commissions, and advertising.
—They offer full-service-bank variety without the capital and regulation, building network effects.
—Fees or commissions are charged to the money-side third parties selling products.
—Credit Karma gave away free credit scores then sold products, acquired by Intuit for $7 billion.
—Borrowell and Fidor Market pivoted to bundle third-party financial products for their users.
