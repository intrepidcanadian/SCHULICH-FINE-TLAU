Pop quiz · reading the borrower.

Do retail lenders favor or disfavor applications with emotion-evoking keywords?
Favor
Disfavor
Example Emotionally charged phrasing — e.g. “I work two jobs to support my family and I promise to repay every cent.” (illustrative)
Result Favor. Retail lenders are more likely to fund applications whose descriptions use keywords that evoke emotion.
Fintech Explained, Ch. 7 · soft-information study (note 11)
Spelling errors, or a description that’s too short or too long — favored or avoided?
Favored
Avoided
Example A one-line request, a rambling three-paragraph plea, or a write-up riddled with typos. (illustrative)
Result Avoided. Lenders shy away from spelling errors and from descriptions that are either too short or too long — they read them as low-quality signals.
