§7.11 · Did online portals disrupt?
30 / 31
Scoring crowdfunding against the five functions of a financial intermediary

Disruption scorecard.

The early hope was that portals would replace banks. Judged on what intermediaries actually do, they succeeded on one dimension, half-succeeded on another, and failed on three.

Reduced ✓
Information asymmetry
More disclosure, transparency, and monitoring — at least for this micro-segment of finance.
Partial ≈
Transaction costs
Cheaper, faster to post a campaign — but still high origination fees and steep rates for low-rated borrowers.
Failed ×
Liquidity
Almost no way to resell a loan or security bought on a portal — you are locked in.
Failed ×
Risk sharing
No skin in the game except balance-sheet lenders. Investors bear the full default risk.
Failed ×
Trust
Same fraud, misrepresentation, and principal-agent problems as the legacy system.
Verdict. Online portals did not disrupt traditional capital raising — they became an additional digital channel for it, carrying some of fintech’s advantages but few of its promised revolutions.
