Pop quiz · algorithms & bias.

Does “alternative data” find hidden-prime borrowers — or do ratings mostly track ordinary credit reports?
Finds hidden prime
Tracks credit reports
Example A platform claims its AI sees creditworthiness beyond FICO — lending profitably to “invisible prime” borrowers banks reject.
Result Contested. Some studies support “invisible prime,” but one study of 4 million US loans found the platform’s ratings were mostly explained by the hard data already in ordinary credit reports.

Identical equity pitch — founder appears Black vs. white. Funded equally or less?
Equally
Less
Example The same project, plan and numbers are shown to backers; only the founder’s apparent race differs.
Result Less. African American male founders are significantly less likely to be funded for identical projects — a gap that shrinks with third-party endorsements and evidence of prior success.
