§7.6 · The rise and fall globally
22 / 28
China built 86% of the world’s market — then it collapsed

A boom, a fraud wave, a crackdown.

2017 · the peak
$358B in China
86% of the $419B global market. 6,600+ portals; $200B from 50 million savers chasing 10%+ returns; $1.2T cumulative.
2016–18 · unraveling
Fraud & Ponzis
Ezubao: $8B raised in two years, 95% of investments fake, 900,000 investors lose $7.6B. Regulators crack down.
2020 · aftermath
84%+ fail
China falls to 1% of global volume ($1.2B). The US becomes the largest market — 65% share, $73.6B.
Fig. 7.1 · Rise and fall of global crowdfundingSource: CCAF
The bigger picture. Despite China’s collapse, most regions kept growing — ~$1.5 trillion raised over eight years at a 33% CAGR, fueled by low rates and institutions’ search for yield. But pioneers pivoted: LendingClub and Zopa became banks; Funding Circle went institutional-only; Kabbage and OnDeck sold to incumbents.
