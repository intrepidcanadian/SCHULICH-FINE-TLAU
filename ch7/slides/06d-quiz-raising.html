Pop quiz · raising money.

Which raises more on average — all-or-nothing or keep-it-all campaigns?
All-or-nothing
Keep-it-all
Example Goal $100,000; only $90,000 is pledged. All-or-nothing returns the $90,000; keep-it-all hands it to the founder.
Result All-or-nothing. It forces founders to bear more risk but raises more on average — and higher-quality, more innovative projects succeed more often.

More
Fewer
Example Before a campaign goes live the platform runs background checks, site visits, credit checks and account monitoring.
Result More. Greater due diligence is associated with a higher share of successful campaigns, more contributors, and more capital raised.
