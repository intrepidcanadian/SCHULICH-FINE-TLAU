Who holds the loan decides who bears the risk

P2P/marketplace vs. balance sheet lending.

P2P / marketplace lender · the intermediary
Matches borrowers with lenders for a fee. Loans funded by retail and institutional investors. Defaults pass through to the investor — the platform bears no loss. Incentives misaligned: it earns origination fees on volume with no skin in the game.
Balance sheet lender · the principal
Acts like a bank, lending its own funds and holding loans on its balance sheet. It bears the credit losses it originates. Incentives aligned with sound underwriting (e.g. Lendified). Many large platforms run both models.
