Pop quiz · how marketplace lending works.

When a borrower defaults on a P2P/marketplace loan, who takes the loss?
The platform
The investor
Example Your slice of a loan to a stranger goes bad. Does the portal that matched you eat the loss — or do you?
Result The investor. The platform passes the loss through and keeps its origination fee — a classic principal-agent problem (misaligned incentives). A balance sheet lender, by contrast, bears its own losses.
Fintech Explained, Ch. 7 · credit risk & incentives (§7.9)
Where does most “peer-to-peer” funding come from today — the crowd or institutions?
The crowd
Institutions
Example The name implies many individuals each chipping in ~$100 to fund a loan.
Result Institutions. Hedge funds, banks and insurers now supply most of the money — about 85% of Prosper’s funds — which is why it’s now called marketplace lending.
