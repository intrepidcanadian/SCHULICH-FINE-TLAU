§4.7b · mNAV, BTC, & the new asset on the balance sheet

A new valuation problem: corporate BTC treasuries, 2020 → 2026.

Slide 13 introduced tokenized RWA. The mirror trade is "tokenized equity": MicroStrategy (now Strategy) accumulating 818K BTC (~$54B basis), financed mostly through ATM equity sales (~97%). Five years on, the valuation question for analysts is mNAV (market cap ÷ BTC NAV). It hovered ~3× through the 2024 ETF launch and has compressed to sub-1× (~0.65×) by late 2025 as $50B mkt cap traded below ~$77B of BTC at spot — making fresh ATM issuance dilutive.

§4.7c · stablecoin issuer enters public markets

Circle IPO · the first big stablecoin float, June 2025.

The tokenized RWA slide (13) framed asset tokenization. The supply-side equivalent is the issuer balance sheet. On Jun 5, 2025 Circle Internet Group (NYSE: CRCL) priced at $31, raising ~$1.05B at a ~$6.9B fully-diluted valuation; the stock more than doubled on day 1 and traded above $200 within weeks, peaking at a ~$60B market cap. The model: USDC float earns short-rate yield; sharing economics with Coinbase + the GENIUS Act (US stablecoin law, signed Jul 18, 2025) reshape the multiple.

§4.13b · bank vs digital-bank multiples

Why a Brazilian neobank trades at 2× JPMorgan’s P/E (May 2026).

Slide 10 (Bank Valuation) anchored a regulated bank to P/E + P/Book on Apr 2023 data. Two years later the spread has widened decisively. JPMorgan trades at ~14.8× trailing / ~13.3× forward earnings (May 2026); Nu Holdings sits at ~24-31× trailing / ~20× forward, despite being a lower-cost, higher-ROE digital bank. The classic textbook answer “banks deserve a discount” is now reversed for neobanks: ROE 33% vs 17%, $0.80/customer/month operating cost, and double-digit deposit growth in LatAm push the multiple. The valuation toolkit didn’t change — the underlying unit economics did.

§4.11b · Finro Q1 2026 416-company dataset

Q1 2026 fintech multiples: the average is 14.5×. The median is 7.6×. Dispersion is the story.

Slide 14 set the 2025 baseline. Finro's Q1 2026 dataset (416 fintechs across 9 sub-sectors) shows the spread has widened, not converged: the 14.5× average masks a 7.6× median — meaning a handful of WealthTech and Blockchain platforms are dragging the headline up while Lending sits at 2.6×. For the discounted-cash-flow lens you applied in slide 8, this is the year you stop quoting “fintech” multiples in the aggregate. The right comp set lives at the sub-sector level — and AI-native & vertical-SaaS economics pull comps closer to enterprise SaaS (~6–7×) than to legacy lending (~2.6×).
