§4.1 · The art of valuation
02 / 23
Negotiation · value is subjective

Valuation is an art, not a science.

Value is what someone will pay. The party in the stronger bargaining position steers the price. For early-stage companies with no revenue history, qualitative factors — team, idea, market — dominate. Traditional models do not work.

The gap between the buyer’s offer and the seller’s ask creates the zone of possible agreement (ZOPA). This is where bargaining takes place.
— Fintech Explained, Ch. 4
Founder trade-off
Ownership vs. Growth Capital
Founders give up equity to raise capital. Understanding the other party’s incentives is the secret to bargaining. Behavioral biases shape negotiations.
Fundamental (intrinsic) value
Bottom-Up Models
Spreadsheets of assumptions about sales, margins, earnings. Analysts can tune inputs to justify any price. Models are negotiating tools.
Relative value
Market Multiples of Comparables
P/E, EV/Sales, EV/EBITDA based on comps. Changing comparables or ratio changes the answer.
