Tab. 8.1 · The US Robo-advisor PioneersSource: Backend Benchmarking
A textbook disruption play · two target segments
Underserved millennials · HENRYs. High earners, not rich yet ($10K or less to invest), tech-savvy, distrustful of banks — and too small for incumbents to serve profitably.
Price-sensitive mass-affluent. Older investors with $100K–$1M who refused to pay high advisory fees for bundled services — DIY by cost, not by choice.
Betterment (2010) was first, soon joined by Wealthfront and Personal Capital (2011) and copycats worldwide: Wealthsimple and Nest Wealth (Canada), Nutmeg (UK), Stockspot and Raiz (Australia).
