The B2B pivot — selling shovels, not gold

Fixing the advisor’s paperwork problem.

Most advisors still use paper forms and wet signatures. Up to 15 hours a week is lost to faulty paperwork, compliance, and admin; ~40% of submitted paperwork comes back for corrections, at ~$15/client per month. Onboarding is the worst pain point of all.

Nest Wealth · Canada’s first robo (2014)
Randy Cass couldn’t match Wealthsimple’s B2C budget after it raised CA$30M. So he pivoted: incumbents wanted not the investing algorithm but the paperless digital onboarding and back-office platform. Nest Wealth Pro signed six licensing partners — including two of Canada’s six largest banks — and B2B now drives most revenue. Its pricing stood out too: a flat $40/month for $40K–$75K accounts (free below, $80 above) rather than a % of AUM.
BlackRock Aladdin · not all innovation is fintech
Aladdin (“Asset Liability and Debt and Derivative Investment Network”) ran BlackRock’s own book, then was licensed to institutions. Aladdin Wealth — now used by 16 retail firms incl. JPMorgan Stanley and UBS — unifies front-, middle-, and back-office, stress-tests portfolios, aggregates household wealth via secure APIs, and simplifies compliance.
[sic] Per textbook: “JPMorgan Stanley” — the author was likely referring to JPMorgan and Morgan Stanley (two separate firms).
