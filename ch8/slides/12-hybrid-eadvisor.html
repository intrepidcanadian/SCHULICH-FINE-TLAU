§8.10 · The future is hybrid
12 / 16
Will robots replace advisors? No — they augment them

Human advisor, machine assistant.

Grace and Sarta argue the future is a hybrid: algorithms handle onboarding, optimization, and reporting; humans handle life decisions and behavioral coaching. EY calls it “Advisor 2.0.” Many investors — especially retirees and baby boomers — still want to talk to a person.

Technology and tools are changing the ways human financial advisors market themselves, interact with clients, optimize processes, and develop and implement investment strategies.
— EY on Advisor 2.0

Tech-enabled eAdvisors are younger, use video conferencing, e-signature, electronic statements, and data-aggregation tools — managing a larger book of clients with more Gen X and Gen Y.
Demographics drive the urgency: retiring advisors aren’t being replaced fast enough to serve an aging population. Productivity from technology is the fix.
