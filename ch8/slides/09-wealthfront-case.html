Wealthfront · founded 2011 by Andrew Rachleff & Dan Carroll

Sophisticated advice for everyone, at low cost.

Rachleff — a VC who taught disruption at Stanford — saw portfolio modeling done in Excel and built software to automate it. After two failed social-investing ideas, the team pivoted to robo-advice and shipped the platform in three months. A questionnaire mapped clients to 1 of 20 risk profiles.

Growth by word-of-mouth. First clients came from a PowerPoint posted on Facebook and LinkedIn, targeting young tech millennials. A referral program managed $5,000 free per invite; one-third of clients arrived this way — letting Wealthfront charge a quarter of rivals’ fees.
The UBS that wasn’t. In 2022 UBS offered $1.4B — $20 per $1 of AUM, $3,000 per client — for the technology, not the fees. After shareholders protested the price, UBS withdrew; Wealthfront remains independent.
Automating the whole financial life. A steady cadence of advice once reserved for the wealthy: daily tax-loss harvesting (2012, +1.6% after-tax), multi-factor smart beta (2017, +0.70%/yr), and risk parity (2018). Plus an automated line of credit — borrow up to 30% of the portfolio, funded within a day — and planning for retirement, college, real estate, and time off to travel.
