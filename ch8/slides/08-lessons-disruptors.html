Why a seven-year head-start was not enough

The math of acquiring a customer.

Acquisition costs $500+ per new customer in advertising. At a 0.35% fee, a $10,000 account earns just $35/yr — a 14.4-year payback. Even a $50,000 account takes 2.9 years. Incumbents simply cross-sell the service to existing customers for almost nothing.

Three takeaways
Customers are the moat
Acquiring customers — not building tech — is the binding constraint for a start-up.
Tech is not a moat
It is copyable. Incumbents out-spend, out-distribute, cross-sell, and acquire (UBS–SigFig, BlackRock–FutureAdvisor).
Work with, not against
Pivot to B2B: Wealthsimple for Advisors and Nest Wealth licensed tech to incumbents to fund growth.
