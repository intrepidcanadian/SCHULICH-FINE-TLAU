Robinhood · 2014 · Vlad Tenev & Baiju Bhatt — Stanford roommates turned HFT quants

Free trades, paid for by your order flow.

Robinhood launched zero-commission trading to democratize finance when discount brokers charged $5–10 a trade — 75% of its first 150K users were born after 1980. Its real revenue was payment for order flow: selling retail trade data to hedge funds.

A fairy tale that did not end well
2014
Launch
$3M seed (a16z, Google Ventures); FINRA broker-dealer approval.
2017–19
Viral
2M→6M→10M users; Series E $323M @ $7.6B. Rivals forced to drop commissions.
Dec 2019
FINRA fine
$1.25M for best-execution failures — trades routed to four order-flow payers.
2020
Exposed
Fortune cover reveals payment for order flow; 12.5M users; Series F–G to $11.2B.
2021–22
GameStop → bust
~3.3M downloads in a month; trading restricted; founders testify. July 2021 IPO at $38 ($31.7B), then −78% by YE2022.
