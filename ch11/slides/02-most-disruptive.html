§11.1 · The most disruptive fintechs?
02 / 13
The threat came from outside the industry, not inside it

Not start-ups — tech platforms.

Early forecasts had agile fintechs disrupting finance. They mostly haven’t: customer acquisition is costly, regulators scrutinize, and incumbents fought back. The real disruptors are the global technology companies — techfins (Alibaba, Tencent) and bigtech (Amazon, Apple, Facebook, Google).

We see over $4.7 trillion of revenue at traditional financial services companies at risk — a $470 billion total profit pool.
— Goldman Sachs analyst report
Two terms · technology first, finance second
2016 · Jack Ma
Techfin
Coined to describe Alipay — the fintech acronym reversed. Tech platforms first, financial services second.
Bigtech
Bigger than banks
Market caps far exceed VISA, JPMorgan, ICBC, and Bank of China. Vast platforms, billions of users.
The pattern
E-commerce → finance
Start with shopping, social, gaming — then add payments, money funds, wealth, and insurance.
