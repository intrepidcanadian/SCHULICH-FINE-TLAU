Filling institutional voids in China’s financial system

Alibaba builds mobile payments.

China had few cards and an underdeveloped payment system. Buyers feared sellers wouldn’t deliver. Alipay (2004) — modeled on PayPal — charged no fees and held payments in escrow until goods arrived, building trust in e-commerce. It wasn’t a revenue strategy; it solved customer pain points.

Built on bank partnerships. From 2005 Alipay signed deals with the 5 major state-owned banks, 15 national banks, and VISA — gaining the payment rails and letting customers pay by card for the first time.
Product innovation. 24-hour hotline, theft compensation, voice and biometric payments, open-sourced mobile WAP, utility bill pay, and the Alipay Wallet with offline QR-code scanning.
