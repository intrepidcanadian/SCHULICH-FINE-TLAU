§11.1 · Why incumbents should worry
03 / 13
Finance as a loss leader, not a profit center

Customer-centric, not product-centric.

Techfins and bigtech don’t sell financial products to make a profit. Finance is an enabler — a loss leader to attract and retain users. Profits come from network effects and the other sides of their multi-sided platforms.

The incumbents’ risk. Banks, insurers, and asset managers are product-centric — they view customers as a source of profit. With platforms owning the user relationship, incumbents risk becoming mere manufacturers of commodity products — “bakers and farmers” fighting for shelf space on marketplaces controlled by Alibaba and Amazon.
The massive fear is you’re beholden to the platform to sell your customer back to you — and take a disproportionate share of the margin. Or worst case, meet that need themselves.
— CEO of Canada’s largest bank
The counter-view. Critics argue the threat is overstated — that these firms simply monetize user data via a thin financial layer, helped by permissive regulation that is now tightening. Like the fintech disruption hype, the threat may fade as techfins and bigtech are brought inside the regulatory perimeter and partner with regulated banks.
